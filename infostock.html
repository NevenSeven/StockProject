Stock Trading Insights

Origins of Stock

The concept of stocks dates back to the early 17th century. The first modern stock market, the Amsterdam Stock Exchange, was established in 1602 by the Dutch East India Company. This allowed people to buy and sell shares of the company, marking the beginning of stock trading. The first stock to be issued in this context was from the Dutch East India Company, which sold shares to raise money for their voyages to Asia.

How People Bought and Traded Stock in the Past

Before modern technology, stock trading was done manually. In the early days of stock markets (especially in places like the New York Stock Exchange), traders would gather on the trading floor. They’d shout and use hand signals to execute transactions. These stock exchanges were physical places where brokers would work directly with investors. For example, in New York, the NYSE was founded in 1792 when 24 stockbrokers signed the Buttonwood Agreement. Trading was done by hand, and you needed to be on the floor or use a broker to facilitate any trades.

How People Buy and Trade Stocks Now

In today's world, trading is almost entirely digital. Investors can buy and sell stocks from anywhere using online brokers or stock trading apps. Here's how people do it:

Brokerages & Apps

Most people use online brokerage accounts or apps to trade stocks. These platforms offer easy-to-use interfaces to buy, sell, and track investments.

Best Apps for Stock Buy & Trading

  • Robinhood – Popular for commission-free trading and an easy-to-use interface, great for beginners.
  • Webull – Provides advanced charting tools, detailed analysis, and no commissions.
  • TD Ameritrade – Offers a comprehensive platform with tons of research tools and educational resources.
  • Fidelity – Known for its low-cost trades and powerful research tools.
  • Charles Schwab – Offers excellent customer service and a variety of investment options.

Market Opening and Closing Times

The U.S. stock market (NYSE, NASDAQ) opens at 9:30 AM Eastern Time (ET) and closes at 4:00 PM ET, Monday to Friday. There are pre-market hours (from 4:00 AM ET to 9:30 AM ET) and after-market hours (from 4:00 PM ET to 8:00 PM ET), but trading volume during these hours is usually lower and prices can be more volatile.

The First Stock

The Dutch East India Company is credited with issuing the first stock (or share) in 1602, making it the first publicly traded company.

Most Valuable Stock

The most valuable stock (by share price) as of recent years is Berkshire Hathaway (BRK.A), the company led by Warren Buffett. As of 2024, a single share of Berkshire Hathaway Class A stock is worth hundreds of thousands of dollars. However, in terms of market capitalization (total value of all outstanding shares), companies like Apple and Microsoft are currently the most valuable in the world.

How Stock Trading Works Today

Stock prices are determined by supply and demand – how many buyers and sellers there are at any given time. If more people want to buy a stock than sell it, the price goes up, and vice versa. Over time, investors use various strategies (technical analysis, fundamental analysis) to predict and capitalize on these price movements.

Stock Market Structure Today

There are two main types of stock exchanges:

  • NYSE (New York Stock Exchange): A physical exchange where stocks of large companies are traded.
  • NASDAQ: A fully electronic exchange with a higher concentration of tech stocks.

In addition to these, there are also over-the-counter (OTC) markets, where smaller companies can trade.

The Concept of Stock Dividends and Splits

Dividends: Many companies pay out part of their profits to shareholders as dividends, usually quarterly.

Stock Splits: When a company’s stock price becomes very high, they may perform a stock split. For example, a 2-for-1 stock split means you get two shares for every one you own, but the price of each share is halved.

Conclusion

The stock market has evolved significantly from its early days of shouting trades to the digital platforms we have now. It's a dynamic space where investors can buy and sell ownership stakes in companies, and with the right tools, anyone can participate. Whether you're looking to build wealth over time with long-term investments or engaging in day trading, the opportunities are vast.
